How does epistasis alter evolutionary trajectories and outcomes?

What's the role of high-order epistasis?

Epistasis can have many effects:

  • Stochastically opens evolutionary trajectories (making evolution contingent on the past).
    Harms & Thornton (2014) Nature 512:203
  • Limits evolutionary trajectories (making evolution follow predictable trajectories).
    Weinreich et al (2006) Science 312(7):111
  • "Entrenches" evolutionary trajectories (making evolution irreversible).
    Shah et al. (2015) PNAS 112(25):E3226

Epistasis: the case of oseltamivir resistance in H1N1 influenza

  • In 1991, the H274Y mutation to an influenza protein was shown to compromise viral fitness
  • In 2008, H274Y entered the H1N1 population and conferred resistance to the drug oseltamivir
  • Between 1991 and 2008, mutations accumulated that offset the deleterious effect of H274Y.


Bloom et al. (2010) Science 328:1272

Conclusions

  • High-order epistatic interactions between multiple mutations are present in experimental genotype-phenotype maps
  • These interactions shape evolutionary outcomes
  • These interactions (may) be a natural consequence of the integrated nature of biology

This result hints at long-term evolutionary memory, where past substitutions alter outcomes over large-regions of the genotype-phenotype map
